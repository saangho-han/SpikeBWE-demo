SPIKE-DRIVEN LOW-POWER SPEECH BANDWIDTH EXTENSION

Donghyun Kim, Sangho Han and Joon-Hyuk Chang †
Department of Electronic Engineering
Hanyang University, Seoul, Republic of Korea
{utahboy3502, vxgkstkdhgxx, jchang}@hanyang.ac.kr
Abstract

Recent speech bandwidth extension (BWE) systems attain impressive fidelity through increasingly deep and complex architectures, but these gains often come with increased computation which costs that limit practical use under power and resource constraints. Therefore, we introduce a spike-driven BWE framework that replaces conventional artificial neural networks (ANNs) with spiking neural networks (SNNs) to bridge gap between performance and implementation complexity. Our experimental results demonstrate that the proposed spike-driven model surpasses the evaluated baselines on speech-quality and intelligibility metrics (e.g., PESQ, ESTOI, and LSD) while maintaining lower estimated energy usage, and superior efficiency.
